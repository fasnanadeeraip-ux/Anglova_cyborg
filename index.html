Anglova CybORG

Scalable Multi-Agent Cyber-Physical Defense Simulation

1/2/4/8/16 Defenders 1-4 Attackers 3 Collision Modes

Overview

Anglova CybORG investigates how scalable Blue defender teams perform against coordinated Red attackers in cyber-physical convoy scenarios. The project analyzes 20 unique defender-to-attacker combinations across three collision modes: spread, all_to_1, and pairwise.

Key Question: How does defense performance scale from 1D:1A to 16D:4A, and what is the impact of collision degradation when multiple attackers converge on single defenders?

Defender-Attacker Matrix

Defenders \ Attackers 1A 2A 3A 4A
1D1:11:21:31:4
2D2:11:12:31:2
4D4:12:14:31:1
8D8:14:18:32:1
16D16:18:116:34:1

Ratios indicate defender advantage. Green zones = high survival probability; Red zones = high compromise risk.

Performance Results

Defense Survival Rate by D:A Ratio

Figure 1: Blue defender survival rate across all 20 D:A combinations. Survival improves with higher D:A ratios, but diminishing returns appear beyond 4D:1A. The all_to_1 mode shows the steepest degradation at low D:A ratios.

Attack Success Rate by D:A Ratio

Figure 2: Red attacker success rate across all 20 D:A combinations. Success drops sharply as defender count increases, with 16D configurations showing near-zero success in spread mode.

Collision Degradation Analysis

Figure 3: Performance degradation as a function of simultaneous Red attackers targeting the same Blue defender. Degradation intensifies with 3A and 4A in all_to_1 mode, showing non-linear decay curves.

Figure 4: Side-by-side comparison of spread, all_to_1, and pairwise modes for 4D:4A. All_to_1 produces the most asymmetric outcome with one defender overwhelmed while others survive.

Scaling Efficiency

Figure 5: Marginal defensive gain per additional defender. Efficiency peaks at 4D for 1-2A scenarios, with 8D and 16D showing reduced marginal returns against 3-4A.

Scenario Timelines

Figure 6: Event timeline for 4D:4A in all_to_1 mode. All four Red attackers converge on one Blue defender, causing rapid degradation while other three defenders remain idle.

Figure 7: Event timeline for 16D:4A in spread mode. Four Red attackers distribute across sixteen defenders, resulting in minimal per-defender pressure and high survival rates.

Heatmaps

Figure 8: Heatmap of defender survival rates across all D:A combinations. Darker cells indicate higher survival. The 16D column shows consistently high survival regardless of attacker count.

Figure 9: Heatmap of Red attack success rates across all D:A combinations. Success concentrated in low-D high-A regions, particularly 1D:4A and 2D:4A.

Resilience Metrics

Figure 10: Average time until first Blue defender compromise. Higher D:A ratios extend time-to-compromise, with 16D configurations showing 3-5x longer resilience than 1D.

Key Findings

  • Diminishing Returns: Beyond 4D:1A, adding defenders yields minimal survival improvement
  • Critical Threshold: 1D:4A and 2D:4A represent high-risk configurations with >80% compromise rate
  • Mode Sensitivity: all_to_1 mode creates extreme variance — one defender fails rapidly while others are untouched
  • Optimal Configuration: 4D:2A in spread mode achieves 95%+ survival with efficient resource use
  • Collision Penalty: Each additional attacker on the same target reduces per-attacker effectiveness by ~15%
